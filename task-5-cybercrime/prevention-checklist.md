Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students in India


Always verify the identity of unknown callers, messages, or emails before sharing any personal or financial information.

Never share sensitive details such as OTP, UPI PIN, passwords, or bank information, even if the request appears official.

While using UPI apps, remember that you only need to enter your PIN to send money, not to receive it.

Avoid scanning QR codes or accepting payment requests from unknown individuals, as they may lead to unauthorized transactions.

Use only trusted and official apps for banking, shopping, and payments, and avoid downloading apps from unknown sources.

Be cautious of heavy discounts, fake job offers, or urgent messages, as these are common tactics used in scams targeting students.

Regularly update your phone and applications to ensure you have the latest security features.

Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts, especially for email and banking apps.

Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions, as it may not be secure.


What To Do When Something Goes Wrong

Incident can be reported on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
